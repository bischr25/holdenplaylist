Holden’s Playlist

Songs that I think are representative of Holden in
The Catcher in the Rye

1. “Creep” — Radiohead

The song’s isolation is the same as Holden’s because they both talk about that they doesn’t fit in anywhere. Like the singer, Holden sees himself as someone with flaws and different from everyone else, even when he hides it behind sarcasm."But I'm a creem, I'm a weirdo, what the hell I'm doing here, I don't belong here"

2. “Piano Man” — Billy Joel

Holden often watches others from the side, noticing their stories and sadness. The song “Piano Man” feels like a musical version of that. It's sympathetic, lonely, and full of small, quiet moments.

3. “Mad World” — Gary Jules

This song shows Holden’s sadness and confusion about adulthood. The lyrics are the same as his constant feeling that the world around him doesn’t make sense.

4. “New York, I Love You But You’re Bringing Me Down” — LCD Soundsystem

Holden loves and hates New York at the same time. This song shows that same love-hate relationship. It’s both critical and nostalgic about the city.

5. “Asleep” — The Smiths

“Asleep” sounds like Holden’s thought of escape. Its sadness show his wish to run away and find peace from the “phoniness” of the world.

6. “Hurt” — Johnny Cash

This song shows Holden’s pain over losing Allie. The emotions and honesty in Cash’s voice are the same as Holden’s grief and his struggle to cope with loss.

7. “Teach Your Children” — Crosby, Stills & Nash

Holden’s dream of being “the catcher in the rye” comes from wanting to protect kids’ innocence. This song amplifies that message. The need for care, understanding, and love between generations all have a direct link to Holden's thought process.

8. “Circle Game” — Joni Mitchell

The song accepts that time moves on and people grow up, which is something Holden fights against. It shows two of the things Holden fears most: change and the loss of innocence.

9. “Landslide” — Fleetwood Mac

To end my playlist, the final song I chose is "Landslide".The song reveals sort of a fear of growing older and losing control, just like Holden’s feelings throughout the book. It’s kind, nostalgic, and has a bunch of self-doubt.
